Mobile Engine Tune-Up Service in Tallahassee, FL

A tune-up addresses the components responsible for your engine's ignition, air flow, and fuel delivery. Over time, spark plugs wear out and lose their ability to ignite fuel efficiently, air filters become clogged restricting airflow, fuel filters restrict flow to the injectors, and belts crack or glaze. The cumulative effect is reduced power, poor fuel economy, rough idle, and hard starts. Tallahassee Mobile Mechanic performs complete mobile tune-up services at your home, office, or anywhere your car is parked.

What's Included in a Mobile Tune-Up

Our standard tune-up service includes: spark plug inspection and replacement (iridium, platinum, or copper per manufacturer specification), engine air filter replacement, fuel filter replacement where accessible (external in-line filters), PCV valve inspection and replacement, serpentine belt visual inspection for cracking and glazing, timing belt inspection where applicable, ignition wire inspection and replacement if needed, throttle body cleaning for improved idle and throttle response, visual check for vacuum leaks, and a full OBD-II diagnostic scan to identify any stored fault codes. We use quality parts that meet or exceed OEM specifications.

How Often Does Your Car Need a Tune-Up?

Tune-up intervals vary significantly by vehicle and spark plug type. Copper spark plugs should be replaced every 30,000 miles. Platinum spark plugs every 60,000 miles. Iridium plugs can last 80,000–100,000 miles on some vehicles. Air filters typically need replacement every 15,000–30,000 miles depending on driving conditions — Tallahassee's sandy roads can accelerate filter clogging. If you're not sure when your last tune-up was, let us check. We'll look at plug condition, filter condition, and any stored fault codes to give you an honest assessment.

Signs Your Engine Needs a Tune-Up

Key indicators that a tune-up is overdue include: rough or lumpy idle — the engine shakes or stumbles at a stop, hesitation when accelerating from a stop or during passing, check engine light with misfire codes (P0300 through P0308), noticeably reduced fuel economy compared to when the car was newer, hard starting especially when cold, engine surging (RPMs hunting up and down without driver input), and increased exhaust emissions. High-mileage vehicles showing any of these symptoms almost always benefit significantly from a proper tune-up.
