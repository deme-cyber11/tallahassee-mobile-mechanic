Pre-Purchase Car Inspection in Tallahassee, FL

Buying a used car is one of the biggest financial decisions most people make — and it's one of the easiest to get wrong. Private sellers don't always disclose every issue. Dealerships make vehicles look clean and drive well for a test drive. Without an independent inspection by a certified mechanic, you're buying on trust and luck. Tallahassee Mobile Mechanic performs comprehensive pre-purchase inspections (PPI) anywhere in the Tallahassee metro area — we go to wherever the car is located.

What Our Pre-Purchase Inspection Covers

Our inspection is comprehensive and includes: full OBD-II diagnostic scan for stored and pending fault codes (many sellers clear codes right before listing), engine compression check where accessible, fluid level and condition checks for oil, coolant, transmission, brake, and power steering fluids, visual inspection for fluid leaks under the vehicle, belt and hose inspection, brake system check including pad thickness and rotor condition, suspension and steering component inspection, tire tread depth measurement and condition evaluation, battery and charging system test, exterior body inspection for rust, repaired accident damage, mismatched paint, or signs of frame damage, interior check including all electrical components and HVAC function, and a road test if the vehicle is operational. We document everything and provide a written report you can keep.

We Go to the Seller — You Don't Have to Arrange Transport

This is the biggest practical advantage of our mobile pre-purchase inspection service. We meet the car wherever it's located in the Tallahassee area — the seller's driveway, a dealership lot, or even at your location if the seller has brought it to you. You don't need to arrange transport of a vehicle you haven't decided to buy yet. We show up, inspect it thoroughly, and give you the complete picture on-site.

What We Commonly Find in Tallahassee Used Car Market

Our inspections frequently uncover: deferred maintenance that sellers didn't mention (dirty engine oil, worn brakes, bald tires), undisclosed accident damage or previous frame repair attempts, fault codes that were cleared before listing to hide problems, oil leaks or coolant leaks that are hard to see without a proper inspection, worn suspension components like ball joints and control arm bushings, and rebuilt or salvage title vehicles presented as clean-title cars. Finding even one of these issues can save you thousands of dollars or help you avoid a money pit entirely.

How to Use Your Inspection Report

After our inspection, we walk you through every finding in person and in your written report. If we find significant issues, you have three powerful options: negotiate a lower price based on documented repair costs, ask the seller to fix the issues before you buy, or walk away with confidence knowing you made an informed decision. Many of our clients report saving $1,000–$5,000 on purchase prices by having documented inspection findings as negotiating leverage.
